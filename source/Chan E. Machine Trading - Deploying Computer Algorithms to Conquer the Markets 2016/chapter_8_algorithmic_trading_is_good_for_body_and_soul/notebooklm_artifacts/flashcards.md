# Trading Flashcards

## Card 1

**Front:** According to the author, why is 'sunk cost' not a valid reason to continue a trading venture?

**Back:** It is not a rational justification for continuing a complex or risky investment of time and money.

---

## Card 2

**Front:** What is the primary motivation for most algorithmic traders besides financial gain?

**Back:** Intellectual challenge at the intersection of mathematics, computer science, and economics.

---

## Card 3

**Front:** Statistics show that the _____ of traders are unprofitable.

**Back:** majority

---

## Card 4

**Front:** Why does algorithmic trading research offer more freedom than traditional scientific research?

**Back:** It requires minimal infrastructure and is unencumbered by bureaucracy or the need for grants.

---

## Card 5

**Front:** Research by Porath (2015) indicates that having a _____ is directly harmful to health and longevity.

**Back:** bad boss

---

## Card 6

**Front:** According to Kahneman (2011), what is the top-most reason for unhappiness in the workplace?

**Back:** Time spent with any boss.

---

## Card 7

**Front:** What health risk is specifically linked to 'too much sitting' by the Mayo Clinic?

**Back:** Increased risk of death from cardiovascular disease and cancer.

---

## Card 8

**Front:** How does owning a pet, such as a Ragdoll cat, benefit a home-based trader?

**Back:** It decreases stress during drawdowns and encourages regular movement through play.

---

## Card 9

**Front:** What is the 'bad optics' equivalent of wearing pajamas at an institutional trading desk?

**Back:** Wearing a headset.

---

## Card 10

**Front:** Why does the author argue that home-based work can enhance productivity?

**Back:** The comfort of home makes working around the clock and on weekends feel less burdensome.

---

## Card 11

**Front:** What was the primary legislative reason for banks scaling down proprietary trading activities?

**Back:** The Dodd-Frank Act.

---

## Card 12

**Front:** An institutional trader's job security typically lasts as long as their cumulative profit is _____.

**Back:** sitting at its high watermark.

---

## Card 13

**Front:** According to Nassim Taleb, how does running your own business make you 'antifragile'?

**Back:** It increases normal fluctuations in income while reducing extreme tail risks.

---

## Card 14

**Front:** How does teaching quantitative techniques act as a synergy for an algorithmic trader?

**Back:** It forces the trader to learn techniques better, which can inspire new strategies.

---

## Card 15

**Front:** What did Richard Feynman find valuable about supervising smart students?

**Back:** It stimulates new ideas and avenues of research.

---

## Card 16

**Front:** How does a foreign currency trader provide societal utility to retail consumers?

**Back:** By acting as the wholesale counterparty behind airport currency counters and ATMs.

---

## Card 17

**Front:** A zero-sum game is considered useful to society if it provides _____ to long-term investors.

**Back:** temporary liquidity

---

## Card 18

**Front:** What is one way investment management provides social utility beyond profit?

**Back:** It safeguards retirement assets for public employees like teachers and healthcare workers.

---

## Card 19

**Front:** What are the two main reasons a profitable trading strategy might stop working?

**Back:** Macroeconomic/market structure changes and competition from other traders.

---

## Card 20

**Front:** Why is 'out-of-sample' testing critical for strategies that look good in backtests?

**Back:** Backtested strategies often fail to perform in live markets due to over-optimization or flaws.

---

## Card 21

**Front:** What is the only occasion where discretion should meet algorithmic trading?

**Back:** Deciding when to start or stop a strategy based on statistical or fundamental evidence.

---

## Card 22

**Front:** How many independent strategies does the author strive to have trading live at any moment?

**Back:** At least 10 strategies.

---

## Card 23

**Front:** The strategy involving the spread of $GLD$ and $GDX$ uses which two metrics for threshold determination?

**Back:** The mean and standard deviation of the spread in a training set.

---

## Card 24

**Front:** What happens to the cumulative return of the $GLD$ vs. $GDX$ strategy if the training set stops in 2013?

**Back:** The cumulative return becomes negative on the test set.

---

## Card 25

**Front:** A Bollinger band strategy for $GLD$ vs. $USO$ typically uses a lookback period of _____ days.

**Back:** 20

---

## Card 26

**Front:** Concept: CPPI (Constant Proportion Portfolio Insurance)

**Back:** Definition: A scheme where the leverage of a strategy is lowered as it loses money until it reaches zero.

---

## Card 27

**Front:** According to Brett Steenbarger, what is a trader's 'ultimate edge'?

**Back:** Perpetual creativity.

---

## Card 28

**Front:** What type of alternative data involves analyzing company executives' voices during calls?

**Back:** Audio analysis to detect gloom, lying, or hidden information.

---

## Card 29

**Front:** Which social media platform is recommended for tracking new quant developments?

**Back:** Twitter.

---

## Card 30

**Front:** What is the function of a 'capital introduction service' offered by prime brokers?

**Back:** Pitching a trader's business plan to potential investors.

---

## Card 31

**Front:** Which online portal offers a full-blown backtesting and live trading environment with investor backing?

**Back:** Quantopian.

---

## Card 32

**Front:** What is the primary benefit of managed accounts (SMAs) regarding investor control?

**Back:** Money stays in the investor's own brokerage account under their control.

---

## Card 33

**Front:** What is 'trading authority' in the context of separate accounts?

**Back:** Permission to execute trades without the authority to withdraw money.

---

## Card 34

**Front:** Why is registration as an Investment Advisor sometimes unnecessary for small managers in the US?

**Back:** Exceptions exist if the manager advises fewer than a specific number of accounts.

---

## Card 35

**Front:** How do brokers facilitate trading for multiple managed accounts simultaneously?

**Back:** By allocating positions from one large order proportionally to account NAVs.

---

## Card 36

**Front:** Why might a managed account structure leak trade secrets?

**Back:** Investors can see the timing and details of every instrument traded.

---

## Card 37

**Front:** What is the 'principal-agent problem' in managed accounts?

**Back:** Managers have no downside risk ('skin in the game'), which encourages excessive risk-taking.

---

## Card 38

**Front:** Trading an account at a leverage higher than the _____ will cause long-term growth to suffer.

**Back:** Kelly optimal

---

## Card 39

**Front:** Why is 'serendipitous idea generation' harder when team members work remotely?

**Back:** Informal, spontaneous exchanges of ideas are more common in physical proximity.

---

## Card 40

**Front:** What online tool is recommended for promoting informal chat among a distributed team?

**Back:** Slack.

---

## Card 41

**Front:** Why is true diversification difficult in a managed account with a small capital base?

**Back:** Small accounts may get zero allocation for strategies requiring low capital amounts.

---

## Card 42

**Front:** In a fund structure, who is responsible for the active management of capital allocation?

**Back:** The fund manager.

---

## Card 43

**Front:** In the US, what body typically regulates a 'commodity pool'?

**Back:** The NFA/CFTC.

## Card 44

**Front:** What is the primary drawback of using backtest evidence alone to start trading?

**Back:** Backtests may suffer from data-snooping bias and fail out-of-sample.

---

## Card 45

**Front:** How does the author characterize the 'shelf life' of trading strategies?

**Back:** Strategies have finite shelf lives and require continuous birth and death cycles.

---

## Card 46

**Back:** It enhances the social utility of the trading by serving institutional public goals.
